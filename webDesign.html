Web Design

1. Include screenshots or mockups:

Be sure to include screenshots or mockups of your web design projects in your portfolio website. This will allow potential clients or employers to get a sense of your design style and the quality of your work.

2. Explain your design process:

Consider including a brief description of your design process for each web design project in your portfolio. This could include information about the research and planning phases, the design tools you used, and any challenges you faced during the project.

3. Highlight key features:

When showcasing web design projects in your portfolio, highlight key features or elements of the design that make it stand out. This could include things like unique navigation menus, eye-catching graphics, or innovative layouts.

4. Use responsive design:

Ensure that your portfolio website itself is designed with responsive design principles so that it can be easily viewed on different devices and screen sizes. This will demonstrate your understanding of the importance of user experience and accessibility in web design.

5. Provide links to live websites:

If possible, provide links to live websites where your web designs have been implemented. This will allow potential clients or employers to see your work in action and get a better sense of how your designs function in the real world.
